About Me

I'm a senior graduate student in the Goodrich and Kugel lab at CU Boulder looking for a postdoctoral position within academia or industry. I am interested in applying bioinformatics approaches to answer mechanistic questions about transcription or RNA biology, in particular circular RNAs. I joined the Goodrich/Kugel lab in May of 2016 and have been working on two main projects:

  1. Understanding Pol II transcriptional regulation upon HSV-1 infection
  2. Investigating the lifecycle and metabolism of B2 ncRNA
I have experience with bioinformatics (ChIP-seq), cell sorting, viral infection, and many in vitro biochemical assays, such as transcription assays, RNA-dependent RNA polymerase extension assays, and gel shifts. Check out the research tab to learn more about my work on both topics!

Outside of the lab, I enjoy reading, attending concerts, making desserts, and the occassional hike. You can peruse my record collection on discogs or take a look at my goodreads. Take a look at some of my adventures!
